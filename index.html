Sean McConoughey is an Aerospace Engineering student at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University specializing in propulsion systems, cyber-physical resilience, and autonomous system security. I currently serve as Public Relations Officer and Electronics Lead for the Experimental Rocket Propulsion Lab (ERPL) and work as a researcher in multiple ERAU cybersecurity and autonomy labs. RESEARCH

Sensor Spoofing Detection for Robotic Systems

Research Assistant – Lead Developer & First Author developing lightweight state-prediction framework to detect sensor spoofing in autonomous systems, tested on GNSS spoofing cases.

Role: Research Assistant – Lead Developer & First Author
Lab: Space Robotics and Generative Estimation Lab
Timeframe: Feb 2025 – Present

Objective: Develop a lightweight state-prediction-based framework to detect sensor spoofing in autonomous systems, tested on GNSS spoofing cases.

Methodology:

  • Built a 4-state EKF for trajectory prediction
  • Implemented funnel-mode Mahalanobis gating around expected state
  • Created derivative monitor for trajectory acceleration anomalies
  • Simulated GNSS spoofing via in-the-loop simulation to evaluate efficacy

Research Impact:

  • First-author paper submitted to AIAA SciTech 2026
  • Supports resilient GNC for autonomous aerospace systems
  • 100% detection rate with sub-second response times in Monte Carlo simulations

Technologies: Python, NumPy, Simulink, EKF, Monte Carlo simulation

Skills: State Estimation · Guidance Navigation & Control · Cybersecurity · Sensor Resilience · Autonomous Robotics

AVIONICS

PANDA Avionics System Development

Electronics design engineer developing next-generation liquid rocket avionics system. Custom PCB features 12 buses, 16 pressure channels, and 12 solenoid channels optimized for high-altitude launch vehicles.

Role: Electronics Design Engineer
Lab: Experimental Rocket Propulsion Lab (ERPL)
Timeframe: Sept 2024 – Present

System Specifications:

  • 12 total communication buses for distributed system architecture
  • 16 pressure transducer channels for comprehensive fluid system monitoring
  • 12 solenoid control channels for valve actuation and flow control
  • 6 load cell inputs for thrust and structural load measurement
  • 6 thermocouple channels for thermal management and safety

Design Approach: Custom PCB design optimized for harsh launch environments and real-time telemetry. Emphasis on redundancy, electromagnetic compatibility, and aerospace-grade component selection.

Applications: High-altitude launch vehicles requiring sophisticated control and monitoring systems with real-time data acquisition capabilities.

Technologies: PCB Design, Embedded Systems, CAN Bus, High-Power Electronics, Aerospace Standards, Signal Conditioning
