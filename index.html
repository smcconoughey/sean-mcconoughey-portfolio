GNSS Spoofing Detection via EKF-Based State Prediction

First-author research developing novel software-only GNSS spoofing defense using Extended Kalman Filter. Achieved 100% detection rate in Monte Carlo simulations with sub-second response times.

Role: Lead Researcher (First Author)
Co-Author: Dr. Cagri Kilic
Timeframe: Feb 2024 ‚Äì Present

Paper Title: "Detecting GNSS Spoofing in Robotic Systems via EKF-Based State Prediction and Derivative Analysis"

Technical Approach: Developed novel software-only defense using 4-state Extended Kalman Filter with funnel monitor and derivative-based velocity checks. Achieved 100% detection rate in Monte Carlo simulations with mean alert times of 0.280 ¬± 0.57s (Mahalanobis) and 0.00015 ¬± 0.0014s (velocity).

Research Impact:

  • First-author paper submitted to AIAA SciTech 2026
  • Primary research project in the Space Robotics and Generative Estimation Lab
  • Positioned as foundational architecture for broader cyber-physical defense systems
  • Novel approach combining traditional estimation theory with cybersecurity applications

Technologies: Extended Kalman Filter, GNSS, Monte Carlo Simulation, Cyber-Resilient Autonomy, MATLAB/Simulink
